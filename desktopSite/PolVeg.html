Traffic Pollution

A high resolution map of annual average (2003) nitrogen dioxide (NO2) concentrations provides an indication of traffic-related air pollution levels within the region.

 

 The map is based upon a land use regression model2 (see reference below) in which air quality measurements are linked to geographic feature describing land use, traffic and topography. The mean nitrogen dioxide NO2 concentration is calculated for the entire route length and route segment concentrations are used to select the least polluted route. Note that the nitrogen dioxide concentrations are best used to determine relative pollution levels for different routes. For reference, the World Health Organization Air Quality Guideline for NO2 corresponds to 21 ppb (parts per billion) for an annual average and 106 ppb for a 1-hour average.

 

Since 2003, levels of nitrogen dioxide have improved slightly (roughly 0.2 ppb decrease per year) in this region (see http://www.metrovancouver.org/about/publications/Publications/2007LFV-AirQualityReport.pdf), but the spatial patterns of traffic-related pollution levels shown in the map are likely to have remained the same since the map was developed, running an the air pollution model with more current nitrogen dioxide data is unnecessary for the purposes of the cycling trip planner.

 

 2 Reference: Henderson SB, Beckerman B, Jerrett M, Brauer M.  Application of land use regression to estimate ambient concentrations of traffic-related NOX and fine particulate matter.  Environmental Science and Technology. 2007; 41 (7):2422 -2428
